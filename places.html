Great Pyramid of Giza

Built for Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BC, this monumental tomb stands as the last of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Its perfect alignment with celestial bodies reveals the advanced astronomical knowledge of our ancestors.

The Great Pyramid was not just a tomb—it was a testament to the ingenuity and spiritual beliefs of ancient Egyptians. Scholars believe its construction involved a workforce of skilled laborers, and the structure was aligned precisely with the cardinal points, possibly to serve religious or astronomical purposes.

  • Location: Giza Plateau, Egypt
  • Height: Originally 146.6 meters (481 feet)
  • Materials: Around 2.3 million blocks of limestone and granite

The Great Sphinx

This colossal limestone statue with the body of a lion and the head of a pharaoh (believed to be Khafre) guards the Giza plateau. Its missing nose remains one of Egypt's great mysteries, lost to time and speculation.

The Great Sphinx is thought to symbolize strength, wisdom, and royal power. Carved directly from the bedrock, it is one of the largest and oldest monolithic statues in the world. Theories about the missing nose range from Napoleon's troops using it for target practice to natural erosion.

  • Location: Giza Plateau, Egypt
  • Length: About 73 meters (240 feet)
  • Height: 20 meters (66 feet)
  • Material: Carved from a single piece of limestone

Temple of Karnak

The largest religious building ever constructed, this vast temple complex in Thebes was built over 2,000 years. The Great Hypostyle Hall's 134 massive columns create a stone forest dedicated to Amun-Ra, king of the gods.

The Karnak Temple complex was developed by generations of pharaohs and served as the heart of worship in ancient Egypt. It reflects the power and devotion of rulers from the Middle Kingdom through to the Ptolemaic era. The Hypostyle Hall is a marvel of engineering, with its towering columns covered in intricate hieroglyphs.

  • Location: Luxor (Ancient Thebes), Egypt
  • Main Deity: Amun-Ra
  • Column Count (Hypostyle Hall): 134
  • Construction Span: Over 2,000 years
