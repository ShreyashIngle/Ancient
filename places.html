Great Pyramid of Giza

Built for Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BC, this monumental tomb stands as the last of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Its perfect alignment with celestial bodies reveals the advanced astronomical knowledge of our ancestors.

The Great Sphinx

This colossal limestone statue with the body of a lion and the head of a pharaoh (believed to be Khafre) guards the Giza plateau. Its missing nose remains one of Egypt's great mysteries, lost to time and speculation.

Temple of Karnak

The largest religious building ever constructed, this vast temple complex in Thebes was built over 2,000 years. The Great Hypostyle Hall's 134 massive columns create a stone forest dedicated to Amun-Ra, king of the gods.

Abu Simbel

Ramses II's monumental temples carved into mountainsides were relocated in a massive UNESCO operation to save them from Lake Nasser's rising waters. The inner sanctum illuminates precisely on the king's birthday and coronation day.
