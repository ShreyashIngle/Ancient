𓂋𓏤𓈖𓇋𓈖𓎡 Wisdom for the Journey 𓎡𓈖𓇋𓈖𓏤𓂋

Sacred knowledge to ensure safe passage through the Two Lands

𓃭

When to Travel

The inundation season (Akhet, June-September) brings fertile soil but difficult travel. Peret (October-February) offers cooler weather ideal for pilgrimage. Shemu (March-May) brings harvest but intense heat.

𓃹

Sacred Dress

Wear light linen garments as our ancestors did. Cover your head during the sun's zenith. Remove sandals when entering temples, and women should carry a shawl for holy sites.

𓃺

Offerings & Conduct

Always approach shrines with respect. Appropriate offerings include bread, beer, or flowers. Never point your feet toward sacred images. Speak softly in places of worship.

𓃻

Navigating the Nile

Travel downstream (north) is faster than upstream. Hire reputable boat captains. Avoid night travel when river demons are most active. Carry offerings for Hapi, god of the Nile.

𓃼

Sacred Transactions

Barter with respect - establish relationship before price. Silver deben is standard, but grain often accepted. Never refuse the first offer in a marketplace.

𓃽

Health & Wellbeing

Drink only boiled or beer (the water is purified in brewing). Eat cooked foods. Carry honey for wounds and cumin for digestion. Seek priests of Sekhmet for healing.
